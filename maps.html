Conflict Maps

Visualizing the geographic distribution of human suffering through data.

1. Regional Impact (Choropleth Map)

This map moves beyond individual events to visualize the systemic nature of conflict across borders. Using a logarithmic color scale, darker shades of red indicate countries where the accumulation of reported fatalities suggests a prolonged state of crisis.

2. The Human Toll (Proportional Symbol Map)

While regional maps show trends, this map focuses on magnitude. Each circle represents a country's total reported civilian fatalities. The size (area) of the circle is mathematically proportional to the number of lives lost, restoring weight to the numbers often lost in aggregate data.

3. Zones of Intensity (Contour Density Map)

Conflict is rarely static; it flows and clusters irrespective of borders. This Contour Map visualizes the density of violence. Using a "Magma" color scale on a dark background, the glowing rings reveal organic "hotspots" of conflict intensity (like in the Sahel or Levant), showing patterns that political boundaries might hide.

Note: Visualizing the geographic density of reported fatalities.
